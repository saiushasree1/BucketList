1.Araku Valley,Andhra Pradesh

Araku Valley is a comparatively unexplored hill station mostly visited by locals as a weekend getaway and is situated some 120 kilometres away from Vishakhapatnam. If you're a tourist looking for exclusivity and tranquillity, Vistadome train leaves Vishakhapatnam at 6.50 AM, making way through 58 tunnels and passing over 84 bridges making way through the breathtaking landscape to reach Araku in approximately in 5 hours, go, catch that!

3.Ellora Caves,Maharashtra

Also known as Verul, Ellora is the name given to a group of Hindu, Buddhist and Jain caves dating back to 6th and 9th century and built during the reigns of Kalachuri, Rashtrakuta and Chalukya dynasties. The site of Ellora is located at a distance of approximately 30 kilometers north-northwest of Aurangabad, wherein lies the district's headquarters.The world heritage site of Ellora caves are made of impressive rock cut temples, forms carved out of formidable hills and several caves dedicated to different sects and cult of Hinduism, Buddhism and Jainism.
